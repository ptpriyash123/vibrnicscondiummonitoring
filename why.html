Why do we do it?


One word: Profitability
Downtime can be incredibly expensive. Loss of production and spoiled product (and wastage)eat into corporate profits. When machine fails, secondary damage occurs. Instead of simply replacing a bearing, the entire rotor or worse may be replaced. The cost can escalate. In addition, the repair time is longer, resulting in longer downtime and higher labor costs. When machine fails, safety may be compromised, spares must be on hand to complete the repair. Holding and managing those spares is very costly. We can achieve this goal through appropriate monitoring. We can achieve this goal through precision balancing and alignment, and through other activities.
